What actually happens in your first psychiatry session — and what to expect

C
Dr. Chirag Aambalia
Founder & Consultant Psychiatrist

Many people avoid seeking psychiatric help because they don't know what to expect. Will they be judged? Will they be put on medication immediately? Will it feel like an interrogation? Here's an honest, detailed look at what a first consultation usually involves — from a psychiatrist's perspective.

Before the session

You'll typically fill in a short form (or we'll ask a few questions when you book) about your main concerns and basic health information. This helps us use the session time well. You don't need to write anything you're not comfortable sharing — the session is the main place we'll talk.

During the first session

Most first sessions last 45–60 minutes. We'll:

  • Understand why you're here — What brought you to seek help now? We'll listen to your story in your words.
  • Ask about your history — Mental and physical health, life events, sleep, stress, and any previous treatment. This isn't to judge; it's to see the full picture.
  • Clarify the diagnosis (if appropriate) — We may explain what we think is going on and answer your questions.
  • Discuss next steps — This might include follow-up sessions, therapy, lifestyle changes, or medication. You're part of the decision; we don't prescribe without your agreement.

You can ask questions at any time. If you're nervous, saying so at the start often helps — we're used to it and will go at your pace.

After the session

We'll usually send a brief summary and any prescription or plan by secure message or email. You can book a follow-up when you're ready.

Taking the step to book a first session is often the hardest part. Knowing what to expect can make it a little easier.
