Grievance Policy

Last updated: February 2025.

Serenest is committed to resolving any concerns or complaints from patients, their families, or referring professionals in a fair and timely manner. This policy explains how you can raise a grievance and what to expect.

What is a grievance?

A grievance may include (but is not limited to): dissatisfaction with the quality of care, conduct of a clinician or staff member, billing or administrative issues, privacy concerns, or any other complaint about our services.

How to raise a grievance

You may submit a grievance by:

  • Email: Send a detailed message to the contact address provided on our Contact page, with the subject line “Grievance” or “Complaint”.
  • Contact form: Use our Contact form and clearly state that you are raising a formal grievance. Include your full name, contact details, and a description of the issue.

Please include: (1) your name and contact information, (2) the date and nature of the incident or concern, (3) the name of the clinician or staff involved (if applicable), and (4) what resolution you are seeking.

What happens next

We will acknowledge your grievance within 3 working days and aim to provide a substantive response within 14 working days. If the matter requires further investigation, we will keep you informed of the timeline. All grievances are treated confidentially and in line with our Privacy Policy.

Escalation

If you are not satisfied with our response, you may request an escalation. We will have a senior team member review the matter and respond within a further 14 working days where possible.

External recourse

You have the right to approach the relevant medical council or consumer forum in your state if you believe your concern has not been adequately addressed. We will cooperate with any lawful investigation.
